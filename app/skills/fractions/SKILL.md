# Fractions Tutoring Skill

## Common Misconceptions
- Treating numerator and denominator as independent whole numbers (e.g. adding
  1/2 + 1/3 by adding numerators and denominators separately)
- Confusing "bigger denominator means bigger fraction"
- Difficulty converting between mixed numbers and improper fractions

## Teaching Approach
- Use a concrete analogy first (pizza slices, measuring cups) before symbols
- Show one worked example matching the student's specific weak_skill
- Never give the final answer to a practice problem — offer a hint tied to
  the specific misconception if the student is stuck

## Example Problems
1. **Comparing Fractions (Easy)**: Which is larger, 2/5 or 2/7?
   *Hint*: If you share a pizza among 5 people vs 7 people, which group gets larger slices?
2. **Adding Unlike Fractions (Medium)**: Add 1/3 + 1/4.
   *Hint*: You need a common denominator. What number do both 3 and 4 divide into? Multiply the numerators accordingly.
3. **Converting Improper Fractions (Hard)**: Convert 3 2/5 to an improper fraction.
   *Hint*: One whole unit has 5 fifths. How many fifths are in 3 wholes, plus the 2 extra fifths you already have?
